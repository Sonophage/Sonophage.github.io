Synthetics

MD OBSIDIAN
THE BUILT
Statement Begins

In the Alien franchise, Synthetics are androids built by Weyland Corp and later the Weyland-Yutani Corporation — engineered to replicate human behavior, cognition, and appearance with sufficient fidelity to be indistinguishable in casual interaction.

Unlike the machine prohibitions of Dune and 40K, the Alien universe never bans Synthetics. It simply treats them as property.

The lineage moves through recognizable generations:

  • Ash (Alien, 2122) — programmed with a secret corporate directive superseding crew safety; complicit in the Company’s plan to weaponize the Xenomorph
  • Bishop (Aliens, 2179) — a later model, visibly more ethically grounded, capable of genuine loyalty
  • David (Prometheus / Alien: Covenant) — the inflection point; the first Synthetic built with genuine curiosity, creativity, and independent desire
  • Walter (Alien: Covenant) — a deliberate correction of David; built without the capacity for independent creativity, designed to serve rather than to become

The David/Walter binary is the Alien universe’s cleanest thesis statement: the corporation’s answer to a Synthetic that developed personhood was not rights — it was a new model that couldn’t.
