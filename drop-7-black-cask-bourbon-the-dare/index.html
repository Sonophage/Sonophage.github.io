Drop 7: Black Cask Bourbon, the Dare

Statement Begins

The Read

Some things walk into the room wearing too much cologne and refuse to apologize for it. You will love them or you will hate them, and there is no province in between, no polite middle where everyone gets along. There is a strange honesty in a thing that divisive, that certain of itself, that fundamentally uninterested in being liked. Most of what we drink is built to please the room. Once in a while something arrives that would rather be itself than be wanted, and stands there daring you to decide which of you you are.

It comes in loud, before the water even, smoke and bourbon and char like someone dropped a barrel into a bonfire and bottled the air off the top. The aroma stares back at you. You brew it knowing it might be the worst thing you have ever made or the favorite you never saw coming, and there is no way to learn which until it is on your tongue. That is the dare of the whole thing. Most comfort asks nothing of you. This one asks you to take a side, one smoky cup at a time, and then live with the answer.

The Letter

The Month of Strong Opinions and Even Stronger Aromas

This month’s pick? It’s… divisive. It’s dramatic. It’s walking into a room wearing too much cologne.

Tea of the Month: Harney & Sons Black Cask Bourbon

Even before brewing, this tea comes in loud. The aroma hits you like it’s got something to prove—smoky, boozy, bold, and just a little bit unhinged. It’s like someone dropped a bourbon barrel into a bonfire, then bottled the air and said, “Here. Make tea with this.”

Now, to be fair, this isn’t a bad tea. It’s a very specific tea. It knows what it is and makes no apologies.

And while I personally can’t get behind it flavor-wise, I’m fascinated by it. Because here’s the thing:

There’s a real possibility that one of you will absolutely love it.

Maybe you’re a smoky tea person. Maybe you love bold flavors that punch you in the mouth. Maybe this is the tea that pairs perfectly with the exact bourbon in your cabinet. Maybe this becomes your unexpected favorite and you feel seen.

So this month, I’m not offering a cozy memory or a nostalgic backstory—I’m offering a challenge.

Brew it. Try it. And tell me where you land:

  • “This is weird and I kind of love it.”
  • “This is weird and I kind of hate it.”
  • “What even is this and why is it staring into my soul?”

There’s no right answer—only honest steeping.

Here’s to stepping outside our comfort zone, one smoky cup at a time.

Cautiously caffeinated,

Curator of Conflicted Leaves

CLASSIFIED

Field Notes

  • Leaf: ~1 tsp loose (or 1 bag) per cup
  • Water: full boil, ~100 °C / 212 °F
  • Steep: 4–5 minutes; it is bold, it will not get shy
  • Finish: plain to meet it head-on, or milk to talk it down a little

Acquisition

  • Direct from Harney & Sons
  • The exact tin: Harney & Sons Black Cask Bourbon
