Heaven and Earth are not benevolent

Heaven and earth are not benevolent; they treat the ten thousand things as straw dogs.

Statement Begins
Heaven and earth are not benevolent; they treat the ten thousand things as straw dogs.

The line is Laozi, Tao Te Ching, chapter 5. Straw dogs were ritual effigies, used with full ceremony and then discarded, held neither in malice nor in favor. The statement asserts nature’s impartiality: the cosmos is not cruel, it is amoral, a frame echoed by Baruch Spinoza‘s Deus sive Natura. Filed against the problem of evil.

See also: Theodicy · The One and the Many · Laozi · Baruch Spinoza
